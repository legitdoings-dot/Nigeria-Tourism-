South-South

  1. Calabar

    2. Experience the festive spirit of Calabar, renowned for its spectacular annual Carnival and rich historical sites. This coastal city offers a delightful blend of cultural celebrations and a glimpse into Nigeria's colonial past.

  2. Obudu Mountain Resort

    3. Discover the beauty and serenity of Obudu Mountain Resort (formerly Obudu Cattle Ranch) Obudu Mountain Resort. Located in Cross River State, it features breathtaking green highlands, a cold, temperate climate, and thrilling attractions like a cable car and a canopy walkway Obudu Mountain Resort.

    Location:

    Obanliku Local Government Area, near the Cameroon border in Cross River State Obudu Mountain Resort.

    Best Time to Visit:

    November through February offers the clearest skies, crisp frosty air, and unobstructed views of the surrounding valleys

    Everything You Need To Know About Obudu Mountain Resort.

    Accommodation:

    The resort offers chalets, African huts, and executive rooms.

    How to Get There:

    It is a 6 to 7-hour road trip from the closest major city (Calabar). You will arrive at the base of the mountain and take the scenic cable car (or a winding 1.5-hour car ride) to reach the peak.

    Key Activities:

    Canopy Walkway:

    A thrilling swing bridge hanging high above the tropical forest.

    Cable Car:

    One of Africa's longest cable cars, offering a stunning ride up to the plateau.

    Water Park & Hiking:

    Natural swimming pools and trails perfect for nature photography and bird watching.

  3. Ikot Abasi

    4. Explore the poignant historical sites of Ikot Abasi, a town that bears witness to the tragic history of the slave trade. Its monuments and museums offer a solemn reflection on this critical period in human history.

  4. Port Harcourt

    5. Discover the coastal charm of Port Harcourt, a major economic hub surrounded by natural beauty. Explore its vibrant city life and venture into nearby areas for opportunities to witness local wildlife.

  5. Gashaka Gumti National Park

    6. Embark on an adventure in Gashaka Gumti National Park, Nigeria's largest game reserve. This vast wilderness is a haven for biodiversity, offering breathtaking landscapes and the chance to encounter a remarkable array of wildlife in their natural habitat.

    Middle-Belt

    1. Jos Plateau

      2. Jos Plateau:

      Be captivated by the breathtaking scenery of the Jos Plateau, a region characterized by its unique rock formations and stunning natural beauty. It's a perfect destination for those seeking tranquility and awe-inspiring vistas.

    2. Lokoja

      3. Stand in awe at Lokoja, where the mighty Rivers Niger and Benue converge. This historic city marks a significant geographical point and offers a fascinating glimpse into Nigeria's natural confluence and past.
