south west

  1. Lagos

    2. Dive into the exhilarating energy of Lagos, Nigeria's bustling economic powerhouse. Explore its world-famous beaches, navigate vibrant markets overflowing with local crafts and flavors, and soak in the infectious rhythm of this dynamic metropolis.

  2. Osun-Osogbo Sacred Grove

    3. Wander through the mystical Osogbo Sacred Grove, a UNESCO World Heritage site. This lush forest is a sanctuary of Yoruba spiritualism, adorned with sculptures and shrines dedicated to the river goddess Oshun, offering a profound connection to ancient traditions.

  3. Ile-Ife

    4. Journey to Ile-Ife, the spiritual homeland of the Yoruba people. This ancient kingdom is a cradle of civilization, renowned for its intricate bronze and terracotta sculptures that whisper tales of a glorious past.

  4. Olumo Rock

    5. Ascend the imposing Olumo Rock, a monumental natural fortress steeped in history. This iconic landmark served as a refuge for the Egba people during inter-tribal wars and offers panoramic views of Abeokuta.
