South-East

  1. Enugu

    2. Enugu: Marvel at the scenic beauty of Enugu, known for its picturesque rolling hills and verdant landscapes. This charming city offers a peaceful retreat and a gateway to the natural splendor of the region.

  2. Owerri

    3. Immerse yourself in the cultural heart of Owerri, a city celebrated for its rich traditions and vibrant arts scene. Experience the warmth of Igbo hospitality and discover the artistic expressions that define this dynamic urban center.

  3. Ada palm

    4. Ada Palm is a massive, government-owned oil palm plantation and refinery located in the Ohaji/Egbema Local Government Area of Imo State, Nigeria. Spanning thousands of hectares, it is one of the largest and most historic palm plantations in West Africa.

  4. Oguta Lake


    5. Oguta Lake (blue/clear water)
    meets the Urashi River (muddy/brown water) in a spectacular natural phenomenon.
    The two rivers flow side-by-side without ever mixing.
    The lake is entirely fresh and free of dangerous sea life like crocodiles and hippos, making it safe for boat cruises.
    The lake also houses a historic, underground bunker built during the Nigerian Civil War.

  5. Azumini Blue River

    6. The Azumini Blue River (locally called Mini-Okigo) is a stunning natural wonder located in Ukwa East LGA of Abia State, near the Akwa Ibom boundary. Famous for its crystal-clear, aquamarine waters, it offers a serene retreat for swimming, canoeing, and picnicking.

    Crystal-Clear Waters: Known as the cleanest body of water in Nigeria, the river is so transparent that you can easily see the riverbed's smooth, colorful stones and swimming fish from the surface.
    Surrounded by lush greenery, the calm waters eventually flow into more tumultuous white water rapids, offering dynamic and breathtaking views.
    It served as an ancient slave trade route and its pure river sand is even utilized by local industries.

  6. Ogbunike Caves

    7. Ogbunike Cave... Finally, Light Beams On 'Abandoned' Heritage.
    The Ogbunike Caves are a network of limestone tunnels and chambers located in a lush, tropical rainforest valley in Anambra State, Nigeria. Discovered by a hunter named Ukwa in the 14th century, the site is a sacred, culturally significant landmark, currently on the tentative list for UNESCO World Heritage Sites.
